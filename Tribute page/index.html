Tribute to Dr.Abdul Qadeer Khan

Dr.Abdul Qadeer Khan also known as A.Q. Khan, was a Pakistani nuclear physicist and metallurgical engineer who is called the "father of Pakistan's atomic weapons program."

Dr.A.Q Khan was born on 1 April 1936 in Bhopal, India. He was a muhajir from India who migrated to Pakistan in 1952. He completed his higher education in Europe, earning a degree in metallurgical engineering from the Technical University of Berlin and a PhD in metallurgical engineering from the Catholic University of Leuven, Belgium.

Khan returned to Pakistan and joined his nation to develop atomic weapons when he founded the Khan Research Laboratories (KRL) in 1976 and was both its chief scientist and director for many years. His return was instrumental in establishing the country's uranium enrichment program, which ultimately led to Pakistan conducting its first successful nuclear tests in 1998.Khan

Dr. Khan's contributions to Pakistan's defense and scientific community have been widely recognized, earning him numerous awards and honors, including the Nishan-e-Imtiaz and Hilal-e-Imtiaz.
